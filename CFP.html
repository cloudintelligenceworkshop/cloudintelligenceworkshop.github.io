Overview

Digital transformation is happening in all industries. Running businesses on top of cloud services (e.g., SaaS, PaaS, IaaS) is becoming the core of digital transformation. However, the large-scale and high complexity of cloud services bring great challenges to the industry. Artificial intelligence and machine learning will play an important role for efficiently and effectively building and operating cloud services. We envision that, with the advance of AI/ML and other technologies, the cloud industry will reach the following while keeping a sustained and exponential growth:

· Highly resilient cloud service. Cloud services will have built-in capabilities of self-monitoring, self-diagnosis and self-healing - all with low human intervention without compromising the service quality nor the user experience.
· Intelligence at users’ fingertips. Users have high trust to cloud services and can easily use a service with few needs for customer support. Vendors can easily use, maintain, and troubleshoot their workloads on top of underline cloud service offerings.
· Highly efficient and effective DevOps. Engineers are empowered with intelligent tools to: (1) build new capabilities of services and smoothly rollout the new capabilities to users; (2) efficiently operate production services; (3) efficiently support users.
The industry is calling for AIOps solutions but is still at a very early stage toward to this vision. We advocate the urgency of driving and accelerating AI/ML for efficient and manageable cloud services through joint efforts in multiple disciplines including but not limited to artificial intelligence, machine learning, software engineering, systems, and software engineering. This workshop provides a forum for researchers and practitioners to present the state of research and practice in AI/ML for efficient and manageable cloud services, and network with colleagues.

Topics

The workshop targets at creating an interdisciplinary forum for researchers and practitioners from fields mentioned above. The workshop encourages submissions of innovative technologies and applications that leverages AI/ML for efficient and manageable cloud services. Topics of interest include but not limited to the AI/ML related techniques, methodologies, and experiences for cloud intelligence and DevOps (Developer and Operations) solutions.

• New design, development, and operational patterns
• Development of cloud services
• Deployment
• System configuration
• Service quality monitoring and anomaly detection
• Resource scheduling and optimization
• Capacity/workload management and prediction
• Hardware/software failure prediction
• Auto-diagnosis and problem localization
• Incident management
• Auto service healing
• Data center management
• Customer support
• Security
• Privacy

Attendance

For each accepted paper, at least one author must attend the workshop and present the paper.

Abstract and Paper Submission

The workshop invites submission of manuscripts with original research results that have not been previously published and that are not currently under review by another conference or journal. Submissions will be assessed based on their novelty, technical quality, significance of impact, interest, clarity, relevance, and reproducibility. Following AAAI conference tradition, submission reviewing is double-blinded. Submitted papers will be peer-reviewed and selected for oral or poster presentation. Accepted papers will be listed on the workshop’s website.

Submissions must be in PDF format, no more than six pages long including all content and references, and formatted in AAAI two-column, camera-ready style (see the 2020 author kit for details)
